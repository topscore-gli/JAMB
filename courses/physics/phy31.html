🧠 Big idea

Current electricity studies how charges move in circuits and how voltage, resistance, and power are related.

1️⃣ Electric Current

Electric current is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor.

Current formula
I = Q/t
  • I = current in amperes
  • Q = charge in coulombs
  • t = time in seconds
Type Description Example
Direct current (DC)Flows in one direction onlyBatteries
Alternating current (AC)Changes direction periodicallyMains electricity

Calculation 1: Current

A charge of 120 C flows through a conductor in 2 minutes. Find the current.

I = Q/t = 120/(2 × 60) = 1 A

Answer: 1 A

2️⃣ Potential Difference

Potential difference is the work done in moving unit charge from one point to another.

Potential difference formula
V = W/Q
Calculation 2: Voltage

If 500 J of work is done to move 25 C of charge, find the potential difference.

V = 500/25 = 20 V

Answer: 20 V

3️⃣ Ohm’s Law

At constant temperature, the current through a metallic conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across it.

Ohm’s law
V = IR
Calculation 3: Resistance from Ohm’s Law

A resistor has a p.d. of 12 V across it when the current is 3 A. Find the resistance.

R = V/I = 12/3 = 4 Ω

Answer: 4 Ω

4️⃣ Electrical Resistance

Resistance is the opposition to the flow of current.

Factors affecting resistance

Resistance formula
R = ρL/A
Calculation 4: Resistance of a Wire

A wire has length 2 m, area 5 × 10⁻⁶ m², and resistivity 1.1 × 10⁻⁶ Ωm. Find its resistance.

R = ρL/A = (1.1 × 10⁻⁶ × 2)/(5 × 10⁻⁶)

R = 0.44 Ω

Answer: 0.44 Ω

6️⃣ Electrical Energy and Power

Electrical energy

Energy formulas
E = VIt = I²Rt = V²t/R

Unit: joule (J) or kilowatt-hour (kWh)

1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J

Electrical power

Power formulas
P = VI = I²R = V²/R
Calculation 5: Energy Consumption

An electric heater rated 2000 W operates for 3 hours. Find the energy consumed and the cost at ₦50 per kWh.

Energy = 2 kW × 3 h = 6 kWh

Cost = 6 × 50 = ₦300

Answer: 6 kWh and ₦300
Calculation 6: Power and Resistance

A 60 W bulb operates on 240 V. Find the current and resistance.

I = P/V = 60/240 = 0.25 A

R = V/I = 240/0.25 = 960 Ω

Answer: 0.25 A and 960 Ω

7️⃣ Resistors in Circuits

Series connection

  • Same current flows through all resistors
  • Total voltage is the sum of individual voltages
Series resistance
Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...
Resistors in series
Series resistors share the same current.

Parallel connection

  • Same voltage across all resistors
  • Total current is the sum of branch currents
Parallel resistance
1/Rtotal = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + ...
R = R₁R₂/(R₁ + R₂) for two resistors
Resistors in parallel
Parallel resistors have the same potential difference across them.
Calculation 7: Series Circuit

Three resistors of 2 Ω, 4 Ω, and 6 Ω are in series across a 12 V battery. Find total resistance, circuit current, and voltage across the 4 Ω resistor.

Rtotal = 2 + 4 + 6 = 12 Ω

I = V/R = 12/12 = 1 A

V₄ = IR = 1 × 4 = 4 V

Answer: 12 Ω, 1 A, 4 V
Calculation 8: Parallel Circuit

Two resistors of 6 Ω and 12 Ω are in parallel across 12 V. Find total resistance, current in each branch, and total current.

1/R = 1/6 + 1/12 = 1/4

R = 4 Ω

I₆ = 12/6 = 2 A, I₁₂ = 12/12 = 1 A

Itotal = 2 + 1 = 3 A

Answer: 4 Ω, 2 A and 1 A, 3 A
Calculation 9: Combined Circuit

Find the total resistance when 4 Ω and 6 Ω are in series, and this combination is in parallel with 12 Ω.

Rs = 4 + 6 = 10 Ω

1/Rtotal = 1/10 + 1/12 = 11/60

Rtotal = 60/11 = 5.45 Ω

Answer: 5.45 Ω

8️⃣ EMF and Internal Resistance

EMF is the total energy supplied per coulomb by a cell. Terminal p.d. is the voltage available when current flows. Internal resistance is the resistance inside the cell.

Key formulas
E = V + Ir
E = I(R + r)
V = IR
Calculation 10: Internal Resistance

A cell has emf 12 V and internal resistance 2 Ω. It is connected to a 10 Ω resistor. Find the current and terminal p.d.

I = E/(R + r) = 12/(10 + 2) = 1 A

V = IR = 1 × 10 = 10 V

Answer: 1 A and 10 V

9️⃣ Cells in Combination

Series connection

Cells in series
Etotal = E₁ + E₂ + E₃ + ...
rtotal = r₁ + r₂ + r₃ + ...

Parallel connection of identical cells

Cells in parallel
Etotal = E
rtotal = r/n
Calculation 11: Cells in Series

Three identical cells each have emf 1.5 V and internal resistance 0.5 Ω. They are connected in series to a 4 Ω resistor. Find the current.

Etotal = 3 × 1.5 = 4.5 V

rtotal = 3 × 0.5 = 1.5 Ω

I = 4.5/(4 + 1.5) = 0.818 A

Answer: 0.818 A

1️⃣1️⃣ Kirchhoff’s Laws

First law: junction law

At any junction, total current entering equals total current leaving.

Current law
ΣIin = ΣIout

Second law: loop law

Around any closed loop, total emf equals total potential drops.

Voltage law
ΣE = ΣIR
Kirchhoff junction idea
Currents entering a junction equal currents leaving it.

1️⃣2️⃣ Wheatstone Bridge and Meter Bridge

Wheatstone bridge

When the bridge is balanced, no current flows through the galvanometer.

Balanced condition
P/Q = R/S

Meter bridge

Meter bridge relation
R₁/R₂ = l₁/l₂
Calculation 13: Meter Bridge

In a meter bridge, the balance point is 40 cm from the left end when R₁ = 6 Ω. Find R₂.

R₁/R₂ = l₁/l₂

6/R₂ = 40/60 = 2/3

R₂ = 9 Ω

Answer: 9 Ω

1️⃣3️⃣ Potentiometer

A potentiometer compares potential differences without drawing current from the source being tested.

Applications

  • Comparing emfs of two cells
  • Measuring internal resistance
  • Measuring small emfs accurately
Potentiometer relation
E₁/E₂ = l₁/l₂

1️⃣5️⃣ JAMB Exam Tips

High-score reminders

  • Always convert units correctly
  • Resistance of metals increases with temperature
  • A short circuit means very small external resistance
  • Ammeters have low resistance, voltmeters have high resistance
  • Maximum power transfer happens when R = r
⚠️ Quick revision checklist

Know the difference between emf and terminal p.d., be able to combine resistors, calculate power and energy, and apply meter bridge and potentiometer relations.
