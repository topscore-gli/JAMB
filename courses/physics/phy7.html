📋 Quick Overview

This topic is one of the most important in JAMB Physics because it connects motion, force, machines, electricity, dams, and even solar energy.

  • Work tells us how much energy is transferred
  • Energy is the ability to do work
  • Power tells us how fast work is done
🧠 Easy way to remember

Work = amount, Energy = ability, Power = speed of doing it.

1️⃣ Definition of Work, Energy and Power

Work

Work is done when a force moves an object in the direction of the force.

Formula for work
W = Fs cosθ
F = force in newtons (N)
s = displacement in metres (m)
θ = angle between force and displacement

If force and movement are in the same direction, then θ = 0° and cos 0 = 1.

Special simple case
W = Fs

Unit of work is the joule (J).

1 J = 1 N m

Simple example

If a force of 10 N pushes a box through 5 m, then:

W = 10 × 5 = 50 J

Energy

Energy is the ability to do work.

Anything that can do work has energy.

Its unit is also the joule (J).

Power

Power is the rate of doing work.

Formula for power
P = W/t
or P = Fv

Unit of power is the watt (W).

1 W = 1 J/s

Difference: Work is total amount done, power is how quickly it is done.

2️⃣ Forms of Energy

Energy exists in many forms.

Kinetic Energy

This is energy due to motion.

Kinetic energy formula
KE = ½mv²

Example: a moving car, a running student, a rolling ball.

Potential Energy

This is stored energy due to position.

Gravitational potential energy
PE = mgh

Example: water stored in a dam, a book on a shelf, a raised stone.

Mechanical Energy

This is the sum of kinetic and potential energy.

Mechanical energy
Mechanical Energy = KE + PE

Other forms

  • Heat or thermal energy
  • Light or radiant energy
  • Sound energy
  • Electrical energy
  • Chemical energy
  • Nuclear energy
🧠 Important comparison

Kinetic energy depends on speed, while potential energy depends on height or position.

3️⃣ Conservation of Energy

The law of conservation of energy states that:

Energy cannot be created or destroyed. It can only be transformed from one form to another.

This means the total energy in a closed system remains constant.

Example of a falling body

A ball at the top has gravitational potential energy.

As it falls, that potential energy changes to kinetic energy.

Just before reaching the ground, most of the energy is kinetic.

Ignoring air resistance
Initial PE = Final KE
mgh = ½mv²

4️⃣ Qualitative Treatment of Energy Transformation

This means explaining how one form of energy changes into another, even without calculation.

  • Potential → Kinetic: a falling object
  • Chemical → Electrical: a battery in a torch
  • Electrical → Heat: an electric iron
  • Electrical → Light: a bulb
  • Nuclear → Heat → Electrical: nuclear power station
  • Potential → Kinetic → Mechanical → Electrical: hydroelectric dam

JAMB tip

  • If the question says “state the transformation,” list the energy changes in order.

5️⃣ Area Under Force-Distance Graph

The area under a force-distance graph gives the work done.

Key idea
Work done = Area under F–s graph

If force is constant, the graph is a rectangle.

So:

Constant force case
Area = F × s = W
🧠 Shortcut

Whenever you see a force-displacement graph, think immediately: “Area = work done.”

6️⃣ Sources of Energy

Energy comes from different sources.

Renewable sources

  • Solar
  • Wind
  • Hydro
  • Biomass
  • Geothermal

Non-renewable sources

  • Coal
  • Crude oil
  • Natural gas
  • Uranium

Renewable means it can be replaced naturally. Non-renewable means it can finish.

7️⃣ Energy and Society

Uses of energy

  • Domestic use — cooking, lighting, cooling
  • Industrial use — machines, factories
  • Agricultural use — irrigation, tractors, processing
  • Transportation — cars, trains, airplanes

Energy and development

Energy is important for national development.

Without energy, industries slow down, hospitals cannot function properly, and schools and homes suffer.

Energy diversification

This means using different energy sources instead of depending on only one.

For example, a country can combine oil, hydro, solar, and wind.

Energy crisis

This is a shortage of energy supply.

It may be caused by depletion, war, poor infrastructure, or too much demand.

8️⃣ Environmental Impact of Energy

  • Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide
  • Carbon dioxide contributes to the greenhouse effect
  • This can cause global warming and climate change
  • Oil spills pollute land and water
  • Nuclear energy may cause radiation problems if badly handled

Important distinction

  • Friendly sources: solar, wind, hydro
  • More hazardous sources: fossil fuels, badly managed nuclear energy

9️⃣ Dams and Energy Production

Dams are usually built across rivers where there is enough water and height difference.

How a hydroelectric dam works

Water stored high up has potential energy.

When released, it flows and gains kinetic energy.

The moving water turns a turbine, producing mechanical energy.

The generator then converts it to electrical energy.

Main conversion
Potential → Kinetic → Mechanical → Electrical

🔟 Nuclear Energy

Nuclear energy comes from the nucleus of atoms, usually through fission.

In a nuclear reactor:

  • Uranium splits
  • Heat is produced
  • The heat produces steam
  • Steam turns turbine
  • Turbine drives generator
  • Advantage: produces large amount of energy
  • Disadvantage: radioactive waste and accident risk

1️⃣1️⃣ Solar Energy

Photoelectric effect

This happens when light falls on a metal surface and ejects electrons.

But this only happens if the light has enough frequency.

Photon energy
E = hf
h = Planck’s constant
f = frequency

Work function

The work function is the minimum energy needed to remove an electron from a metal surface.

Work function relation
φ = hf₀
f₀ = threshold frequency

If the light frequency is below the threshold frequency, no electrons are emitted.

Solar collector

A solar collector absorbs sunlight and converts it to heat, for example in water heating.

Solar panel

Solar panels use photovoltaic cells to convert light directly into electrical energy.

Main conversion in solar panel: Light energy → Electrical energy

1️⃣3️⃣ 10 JAMB-Style Examples with Step-by-Step Solutions

1. If the force and the velocity on a system are each reduced simultaneously by half, the power of the system is

A. doubled B. reduced to a quarter C. reduced by half D. constant

Step 1: Recall P = Fv

Step 2: New force = F/2 and new velocity = v/2

Step 3: New power = (F/2)(v/2) = Fv/4

Step 4: So power becomes one-quarter

Answer: B. reduced to a quarter
2. A car of mass 800 kg attains a speed of 25 m/s in 20 s. The power developed in the engine is

A. 1.25 × 10⁴ W B. 2.50 × 10⁴ W C. 1.25 × 10⁶ W D. 2.50 × 10⁶ W

Step 1: Use rate of change of kinetic energy

Step 2: KE = ½mv² = ½ × 800 × 25²

Step 3: KE = 400 × 625 = 250,000 J

Step 4: P = W/t = 250,000 / 20

Step 5: P = 12,500 W = 1.25 × 10⁴ W

Answer: A. 1.25 × 10⁴ W
3. The work done by a force in moving an object over a distance of 4 m is 48 J. If the force is doubled, the work done over the same distance would be

A. 24 J B. 48 J C. 96 J D. 192 J

Step 1: W = Fs

Step 2: If force doubles and distance remains the same, work also doubles

Step 3: New work = 2 × 48 = 96 J

Answer: C. 96 J
4. A machine whose efficiency is 75% is used to lift a load of 1000 N. If the velocity ratio of the machine is 4, find the effort required.

A. 187.5 N B. 333.3 N C. 533.3 N D. 1333.3 N

Step 1: Efficiency = (MA/VR) × 100

Step 2: 75 = (MA/4) × 100

Step 3: MA = 3

Step 4: MA = Load / Effort

Step 5: Effort = 1000 / 3 = 333.3 N

Answer: B. 333.3 N
5. A body falls from a height of 20 m. What is its kinetic energy just before hitting the ground? (g = 10 m/s², mass = 2 kg, ignore air resistance)

A. 200 J B. 400 J C. 600 J D. 800 J

Step 1: By conservation of energy, PE = KE

Step 2: PE = mgh = 2 × 10 × 20

Step 3: PE = 400 J

Step 4: Therefore KE = 400 J

Answer: B. 400 J
6. Which of the following is a renewable source of energy?

A. Coal B. Crude oil C. Wind D. Nuclear

Step 1: Renewable sources are naturally replenished

Step 2: Wind is renewable

Answer: C. Wind
7. The environmental impact of burning fossil fuels includes

A. Reduction in global warming B. Greenhouse effect C. Energy diversification D. Safe energy use

Step 1: Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide

Step 2: Carbon dioxide causes greenhouse effect

Answer: B. Greenhouse effect
8. In a hydroelectric dam, the energy conversion is mainly

A. Kinetic to potential B. Potential to kinetic to electrical C. Chemical to electrical D. Nuclear to heat

Step 1: Water stored high up has potential energy

Step 2: As it flows, it gains kinetic energy

Step 3: Turbine and generator finally produce electrical energy

Answer: B. Potential to kinetic to electrical
9. The work function of a metal is 3.2 eV. What is the threshold frequency? (h = 4.14 × 10⁻¹⁵ eV s)

A. 7.7 × 10¹⁴ Hz B. 5.5 × 10¹⁴ Hz C. 9.9 × 10¹⁴ Hz D. 4.4 × 10¹⁴ Hz

Step 1: Use φ = hf₀

Step 2: f₀ = φ/h = 3.2 / (4.14 × 10⁻¹⁵)

Step 3: f₀ ≈ 7.7 × 10¹⁴ Hz

Answer: A. 7.7 × 10¹⁴ Hz
10. A solar panel uses the photoelectric effect to convert

A. Heat to electrical energy B. Light to electrical energy C. Kinetic to potential energy D. Chemical to light energy

Step 1: Solar panels use light from the sun

Step 2: Photovoltaic cells convert light directly into electrical energy

Answer: B. Light to electrical energy
