🧠 One-line recall

Bepo corrected the school’s bad grammar, later witnessed a ritual at 2:51 a.m., and left because he could not accept such evil.

1️⃣ Flashback Setting: Beesway Before Stardom

This chapter is a flashback to Mr. Bepo Adewale’s earlier job at Beesway Group of School, located on the outskirts of Lagos, before he joined Stardom Schools.

  • The chapter explains why he left Beesway
  • It shows his principles, courage, and attention to detail

2️⃣ The Grammar Dispute

Bepo notices that the school’s name is grammatically wrong.

“Beesway Group of School”
— Wrong form used by the school
“Beesway Group of Schools”
— Correct form suggested by Bepo

Why? “Group of” suggests more than one, so the noun should be plural.

“Like saying, ‘a group of man’ when we mean ‘a group of men’.”
— Bepo’s analogy for the error

3️⃣ Director’s Reaction

The director, Mr. Egi Meko, reacts mockingly and refuses to change the name.

“divinely inspired”
— Mr. Egi Meko’s excuse for keeping the wrong school name
  • He says the name is already registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC)
  • He treats Bepo’s correction as unnecessary

4️⃣ The Main Shock: Ritual at Night

One night, Bepo witnesses a horrifying scene on the school premises.

“2:51 a.m.”
— Exact time of the ritual incident

What he sees

  • Mr. Egi Meko and other men are burying a cow alive
  • The act appears ritualistic, meant for success/protection/spiritual power

5️⃣ Bepo Tries to Stop Them

Bepo attempts to intervene because he sees the act as cruel and wrong.

“right wrist with a club”
— Where/how he is attacked
“his own machete”
— Weapon used to threaten him

This violent response confirms to Bepo that Beesway is not a place where integrity and morality are respected.

6️⃣ Why Bepo Leaves Beesway

The ritual incident strengthens the disagreement he already has with management.

  • He cannot tolerate cruelty, superstition, and unethical behaviour
  • He believes success should come from hard work, not dark practices
  • This is what pushes him toward Stardom Schools

7️⃣ Themes & Lessons

  • Moral integrity: Bepo refuses to compromise
  • Superstition vs hard work: some seek success through rituals instead of effort
  • Courage: Bepo speaks up even when it is risky
  • Corruption in education: bad practices can exist behind polished school images
🧠 Theme line

This chapter proves that Bepo is principled: he may bend for peace, but not for evil.
